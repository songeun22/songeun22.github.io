Classical statistics leans on strong assumptions, a known model family, correct specification, i.i.d. data, and this book starts by loosening them.

Push that far enough and you end up willing to treat a model as one black box machine, no promises about what's inside, no guarantee it's the "right" model for anything. The question this book actually starts from is what's left to say once you've given that up: how do you still get theoretically grounded statistics out of a system you're not allowed to look inside?

Conformal prediction and the martingale posterior are two different answers to that question, and this book works through both.
