Once you're studying ML, high-dimensional statistics turns out to be impossible to avoid. It's the thing quietly underneath a lot of "why does this work" questions, why regularization matters, why more parameters than samples isn't automatically a disaster, why some estimators stay well-behaved and others fall apart.

There's no course at my school that covers this directly, so this book is me working through it from the materials below and writing up what I understand as I go. The throughline: studying how traditional statistical methods break down once the setting turns high-dimensional, and what has to change to fix them.

## Materials

- MIT 18.657, [High-Dimensional Statistics](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2310.19244), lecture notes by Philippe Rigollet and Jan-Christian Hütter.
