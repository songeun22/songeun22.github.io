The lasso is great for point estimation in high dimensions, but the regularization that makes it work also biases the estimates in a way that breaks ordinary confidence intervals. The debiased, or desparsified, lasso is one answer to that: correct the bias back out and recover something close to asymptotically normal, so inference is possible again.
