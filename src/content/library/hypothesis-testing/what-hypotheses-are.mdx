Before pulling anything apart, it's worth being explicit about the standard picture everyone starts from.

Take a few familiar settings: a clinical trial testing a new drug against a placebo, a screening test trying to catch a disease early, a lab comparing two versions of an assay before shipping either one. In every one of these, someone has to decide, from noisy data, whether an effect is real or whether they're staring at noise.
